3D Printing

Introduction to 3D Printing

  • 3D printing, also known as additive manufacturing, is a process of creating three-dimensional objects from a digital file
  • It works by adding material layer by layer until the object is formed, unlike traditional subtractive manufacturing methods where material is removed from a block.

Types of 3D Printing Technologies:

  • Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM): Most common type, where thermoplastic filaments are melted and extruded through a nozzle.
  • Stereolithography (SLA): Uses a UV laser to cure liquid resin into solid layers.
  • Selective Laser Sintering (SLS): Utilizes a laser to sinter powdered material, such as metal or nylon, into solid layers.
  • Digital Light Processing (DLP): Similar to SLA but uses a digital light projector to cure resin.

Applications:

  • Prototyping: One of the earliest and still most prevalent uses of 3D printing is rapid prototyping, enabling designers to quickly iterate on designs.
  • Manufacturing: 3D printing is increasingly used in small-scale manufacturing for customized or low-volume production.
  • Medicine and Healthcare: From prosthetics to surgical tools to personalized implants, 3D printing is revolutionizing healthcare.
  • Education: It's used in schools and universities to teach design, engineering, and manufacturing concepts
